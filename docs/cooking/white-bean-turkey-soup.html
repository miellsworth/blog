Lemony White Bean Soup With Turkey and Greens

Turkey
Soup
Author

New York Times

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion
  • 1 large carrot
  • 1 bunch sturdy greens (kale, broccoli rabe, mustard greens or collard greens)
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 3/4 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/8 tsp red-pepper flakes
  • 250 g ground turkey
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1 tbsp finely grated ginger
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • 1 L chicken stock
  • 2 cans (400 ml) white beans
  • 1 cup chopped fresh, soft herbs (parsley, mint, dill, basil, tarragon, chives or a combination)
  • Fresh lemon juice

Instructions

0.5 Prepare ingredients

  • Dice onion
  • Dice carrot
  • Mince garlic
  • Drain and rinse beans
  • Rinse greens, pull leaves off stems, tear into bite-size pieces

1 Saute veggies

  • Heat a large pot over medium-high for a minute or so to warm it up
  • Add the oil and heat until it thins out, about 30 seconds
  • Add onion and carrot, and sauté until very soft and brown at the edges, 7 to 10 minutes

2 Cook the turkey

  • When the onion is golden, add tomato paste, 3/4 tsp cumin and 1/8 tsp red-pepper flakes to the pot, sauté until paste darkens, about 1 minute
  • Add turkey, garlic, ginger and 1 teaspoon salt, and sauté, breaking up the meat with your spoon, until turkey is browned in spots, 4 to 7 minutes

3 Add stock and beans

  • Add stock and beans, and bring to a simmer
  • Let simmer until the soup is thick and flavorful, adding more salt if needed, 15 to 25 minutes

NOTE: If you like a thicker broth, you can smash some of the beans with the back of the spoon to release their starch. Or leave the beans whole for a brothier soup.

4 Add greens

  • Add the greens to the pot and simmer until they are very soft
  • This will take 5 to 10 minutes for most greens, but tough greens might take 15 minutes
  • Add a little water if the broth gets too reduced

5 Add herbs and serve

  • Stir herbs and lemon juice into the pot
  • Taste and add more salt, cumin and lemon until the broth is lively and bright-tasting
  • Serve topped with a drizzle of olive oil and more red-pepper flakes, if desired
